Structural audit · Hickey × Lowy

What in kolu is electricity?

A receptacle hides enormous volatility — AC/DC, 110/220V, 50/60Hz — behind one standardized socket. You don't reinvent it per appliance; you plug in and add value. We already did this once, for @kolu/surface. This audit asks what's left.

01

The electricity test

Lowy's metaphor is not "anything reusable." A genuine receptacle clears three bars at once. Most "utilities" clear only the first.

① Domain-agnostic

Nothing about terminals · canvas · agents · git leaks into the interface. The Hickey check: would extracting it dissolve a braid, or merely move it across a package wall?

② Encapsulates a volatility

It hides a real axis of change — browser-API churn, transport, persistence corruption. A pure 3-line === loop hides nothing that will ever change; that's not electricity.

③ Cross-cutting reuse

The same need appears in many places — ideally across packages, the way surface/pty-host plug into a different app. One consumer is a function, not a utility.

The receptacle is already installed across most of kolu's high-volatility axes. These own their churn and rarely leak domain — the bar every new candidate is measured against:

@kolu/surface · snapshot-then-delta transport @kolu/pty-host · multi-client PTY @kolu/artifact-sdk · text-anchoring integrations/io · refcounted fs.watch integrations/git · github solid-fileview · markdown · pierre transcript-core · html Corvu · dialog/drawer/focus-trap/scroll-lock @solid-primitives · ResizeObserver/matchMedia/storage

A correction on "premature"

The first cut of this report filed seven candidates as premature — "the second consumer isn't here yet, extract later." That rationale rests on lock-in: once you publish a package, callers freeze against it and the shape is expensive to fix. It barely applies to kolu. You own every package and every call site, so an interface and all its consumers move in one atomic commit — "wait for the rule-of-three to learn the shape" is a hedge against a reversal cost you don't pay.

So the temporal verdict is dropped. What survives is purely structural, and it doesn't improve with consumer count: five of the seven would complect (move a braid, fuse two policies behind a config-bag, leak domain) or hide no volatility at all — wrong whenever you built them; two are genuinely clean and just not yet load-bearing — fine to extract now, the only cost being one more import edge. The rule for an owned monorepo isn't "wait for N consumers" — it's "extract when the boundary is simple; don't when it would complect — regardless of count."

kolu is ~80% thin domain-composition over good electricity, not a tangle. The big extraction already happened — surface ate the hardest volatility (client↔server state) end-to-end. What remains is mostly relocations and leaf-type packages, two real decompositions, and a handful of bugs wearing an architecture costume. Exactly one candidate is clean, new electricity — and it isn't the one anyone guessed.
03

What's still braided

The accidental complexity is concentrated, not pervasive. Six themes recur — and one is the cheapest high-value fix in the whole repo.

Creation implies activation biggest braid · cheapest fix

handleCreate unconditionally calls store.setActiveSilently — so "make a terminal" and "make it active" are welded. The only caller that wants creation without activation (session restore) must fight back with a documented three-step protocol braiding identity, create-order, and canvas-pan.

make terminal  ⊗  select active  ⊗  pan viewport  ⊗  fire contextual tip

ev useTerminalCrud.ts:145 · useSessionRestore.ts:179-301 — the code's own comment admits the protocol "collapses to step 2 alone the day handleCreate accepts an activate:false flag (TODO)." One flag dissolves it.

Optimistic-local vs. server-echo, braided with identity + time

A single value (a tile's rect; the active terminal) exists in two representations whose "which is true" is a function of time + an equality check — re-threaded by hand through canvas, session-restore, and CRUD. This is the mechanical seam behind the candidate the synthesis under-ranked (see §05).

local pending value  ⊗  server-saved value  ⊗  equality-gated reconcile  ⊗  per-key lifecycle

ev usePendingLayouts.ts:86-106 dropEvicted · TerminalCanvas.tsx:136-156 layoutOf merges pending over saved.

God-procedures: every concern under one owner

Independently-extractable blocks share one un-touchable unit, so touching one concern forces re-reading the whole web.

Terminal.tsx onMount — ~500 lines: xterm ctor ⊗ WebGL ⊗ mobile input ⊗ keyboard ⊗ PTY stream ⊗ resize ⊗ touch-scroll ⊗ upload ⊗ owner-restore

ev Terminal.tsx:443-950 · CodeTab.tsx (~800 lines, 5 mutually-aware reactive invariants) · App.tsx (~700) · index.ts (307-line boot, no app-factory seam).

One concept, expressed two+ ways — drifting apart

The same idea hand-rolled repeatedly, free to diverge.

bounding-box accumulation ×5 · the Logger contract re-typed in 3 packages · ~9 structural-equality comparators · two parallel agent-state bucket enums (4-bucket vs 5-bucket) · isStale's boundary written twice (> vs >=)

ev the isStale off-by-one is real, papered over only by forced call-ordering at staleness.ts:84.

The query that is secretly a command

A "pure" signature that quietly mutates — load-bearing prose substituting for a clean seam.

placeNew documented as a pure-query shape, but MAY mutate siblings + dispatch RPCs before returning · makeHooks takes a record arg it discards (_record) for a hypothetical second host

ev TerminalCanvas.tsx:82-87 — the hidden side-effect contract lives in the comment, not the type.

Switch-on-type where data would do

String-literal special-casing and match() ladders where a registry would let new cases register without editing the transport file.

useShortcuts special-cases two action ids by literal · router's exportTranscriptHtml hard-codes match(agent).with(…) where a Record<agentKind, loader> belongs

ev mechanism braided with policy: screenshotTerminal welds pure xterm-buffer→color decoding to kolu window-chrome/branding in one 210-line fn.

04

The boundaries hold

A Lowy read of the module walls — where volatility is encapsulated, and where it has leaked or never finished encapsulating.

The gold-standard receptacles genuinely own their volatilities and rarely leak domain: surface's Cell/Collection are id-opaque; artifact-sdk's anchoring core speaks only Range/Document/ShadowRoot; the terminalBackend location.kind dispatch is ready for the SSH-host variant. Whole axes the brief flagged as commonly-missed — focus/keyboard-scope, persisted storage, ResizeObserver, toast (deliberately colocated per toast-conventions.md), URL/route state (doesn't exist in a single-page canvas app) — are not missing electricity. They're already plugged, often by leaning on Corvu and @solid-primitives rather than hand-rolling.

The remaining friction clusters in four named patterns, not a pervasive mess:

Mislocation, not duplication

Genuinely agnostic primitives live under feature folders and get cross-imported — proof they outgrew their home. walkShadowRoots lives under comments/ but pierreTouchScroll reaches across for it; capturePointerGesture is clean electricity trapped unexported in canvas/viewport/. The fix is a neutral folder wall, not new abstraction.

Leaf-package policy forces re-typing

io and transcript-core re-declare the canonical Logger privately to avoid depending on kolu-shared; transcript-html pulls the whole kolu-common domain package just to reach escapeHtml. The missing receptacle is a zero-dep leaf every package can import.

A baked-in concretion blocks reuse

createDebounceWatcher welds Db extends Closable into a generic debounce frame, so JSONL-tail and git watchers can't use it and re-hand-roll the loop. The file-watcher mechanism is extracted twice into non-interoperating families (WAL vs dir) plus three hand-rolls. Pull the DB out and one factory serves all sources.

Half-applied boundaries

A docstring claims cross-surface reuse the code never delivers. tileChrome says its color-mix formula flows to workspace pills — those hand-roll different recipes and never import it. useActivityAlerts claims "all output channels live here" — navigator.setAppBadge stayed inline. Decomposition started, not finished.

Also flagged: speculative seams (premature Lowy) — makeHooks's discarded record arg, getTerminalBackendFor's no-op resolver stub, ChromeBar's right-panel-width invariant "maintained by convention across two files." Fine if the second case arrives; pure carrying-cost if it never does.

06

What the synthesis missed

A completeness critic re-swept for cross-cutting electricity no single reader would own. It overrode the backlog twice — both in the file-watcher / optimistic-state neighborhood, which is exactly where the biggest braids live.

★ optimisticOverlay — the candidate the backlog under-ranked to invisibility

An id-keyed optimistic-write-with-echo reconciliation primitive. Parameterized only on an opaque id, a value type, an equality predicate, and a saved(id) reader — nothing terminal/canvas-shaped is intrinsic. dropEvicted garbage-collects a pending entry once the saved value catches up (or its key dies); a fine-grained createStore keeps per-key reactivity.

This is the mechanical core of the single biggest remaining complecting theme (§03) — the "optimistic local vs. server echo" need that recurs for any rename / reorder / comment-position. It's the natural neighbor of surface's Cell/Collection reconcilers, which own server-reconcile but not optimistic-overlay-until-echo. Rate plausible-to-strong.

ev usePendingLayouts.ts:25-111 · consumers TerminalCanvas.tsx:136-156, useCanvasArrange.ts:54-63, useSessionRestore.ts:119.   note strip the window.__koluPendingApplyHistory e2e ring buffer before extracting.

installOnceFanOut — the watcher count is three+, not "twice"

The fan-out half of @kolu/fs-watch — lazy-install-once-on-first-registrant, broadcast to N listeners inside a per-listener try/catch fence, snapshot-before-iterate — is independently re-expressed at least three times: io/refcounted-dir-watcher, git/working-tree-watcher (the family the backlog didn't enumerate), and providers.ts's activations Map. It's the in-process broadcast cousin of surface's Channel fan-out — and reinforces that the file-watcher zoo is the clearest "we wrote this 3+ times" smell in the codebase.

ev io/refcounted-dir-watcher.ts:76-110 · git/working-tree-watcher.ts:94-120 · providers.ts:320-347, 416-437.

The critic also pruned the backlog: canvas-leak-ledger, command-palette, persistedPref's migration half, and createSharedRoot's "promotion" were flagged as padding or YAGNI — already reflected in the verdicts above.

07

What to actually do

The "ship now" tier has landed in #1089 (first tier below). What remains, ordered by confidence-per-effort: two real decompositions, a few in-place fixes, then the don't-build / your-call backlog — the last two split on whether the objection is structural (stands) or merely temporal (your call).

✓ shipped · #1089

Landed — the genuine receptacle, two leaf packages, the relocations

  • persistedPref ✓ — validate-on-read receptacle over makePersisted, applied to all 5 prefs; closed the zoom NaN and maximize "false"-truthy bugs. Review tightened parse to single-mode (always throw on invalid) so onInvalid can't be silently bypassed.
  • @kolu/log + @kolu/html-escape ✓ — zero-dep leaf packages. io + transcript-core stopped re-typing Logger (kolu-shared re-exports), and transcript-html dropped its kolu-common dependency entirely.
  • Relocations ✓ — walkShadowRoots → neutral dom/; isMac made injectable in the keybind-core. capturePointerGesture turned out to already be an exported, reused module — a no-op, flagged honestly rather than invented work.
  • In-PR follow-ups: single-pass escapeHtml (police), the boolean serialize/parse contract documented (lowy cross-validate), and a default.nix src-fileset fix CI caught — the new package dirs weren't in the curated allowlist, which just check can't see.
next ▸ decompose

Real need — reshape into the right seam, not a grab-bag

  • fs-watch → two layers — refcountedWatcher (L0, hides the watch backend) + gatedReader (L1, DB-decoupled). The clearest server-side "wrote it thrice" smell; folds in installOnceFanOut.
  • optimisticOverlay — the missed primitive. Extract the dropEvicted reconciler next to surface's Cell/Collection. It dissolves the costliest client braid and serves every future rename/reorder/comment-position.
fix in place

A bug or a braid wearing an architecture costume

  • Add handleCreate(…, {activate:false}) — the single cheapest high-value change in the report. It collapses the 3-step session-restore protocol to one step and un-welds creation from activation.
  • Collapse the isStale off-by-one (> vs >=) — a one-file Hickey fix, not an extraction.
  • Break up the god-procedures — Terminal.tsx onMount, CodeTab.tsx, index.ts boot. Pure organization; each block is already independently extractable.
don't build — at any count

Wrong shape regardless of how many of your apps consume it

  • geometry · canvas-leak-ledger · command-palette extraction — the interface can't shed the domain: a Rect package re-adapts a CanvasLayout at every boundary; the leak-ledger's vocabulary is xterm-addon internals; the palette engine bakes in five kolu couplings. Extracting moves the braid, it doesn't dissolve it — and owning the call sites doesn't change a structural fact.
  • createRovingCursor — would complect two genuinely different selection policies (index/reset vs id/preserve, 1D vs 2D) behind a config-bag carrying more surface than the two call sites have code. Two ~15-line local cursors are simpler than one knob-laden primitive, at 2 consumers or 200.
  • @kolu/equality — nothing to hide: === / .length / a for-loop have zero churn, so there's no volatility to encapsulate. At most fold nullableEqual into the existing @kolu/nonempty.
your call — clean, not yet

No structural objection — extract whenever, it's cheap because you own everything

  • @kolu/lineref — agnostic, zero-import parse/format; today only a type is shared, so a module beats a package. Promote when a second free-text surface wants the grammar.
  • @kolu/static-serve — clean signatures; the only blocker is the kolu-URL / kolu-git invariant coupling, also yours to move. No payoff at one caller, but no reason you can't the day a second file route appears.
  • Both are "extract on the rule-of-three if you like" — but since reversal is a single atomic commit here, even that heuristic is optional. Don't extract for its own sake; do it when a real second consumer makes the seam pull its weight.
The "prefer external libraries" rule is already paying off: Corvu and @solid-primitives quietly own most of the browser-API volatility a less disciplined codebase would have hand-rolled into a dozen bespoke spots. With the validated-storage receptacle and the two leaf-type packages now shipped in #1089, the remaining electricity is just two real decompositions (fs-watch, optimisticOverlay) and a flag named activate.
