Electricity — identified, and their progress

A tracker for kolu's electricities: infrastructure pulled out from beneath the app into its own thing. evergreen

What counts as electricity (Löwy's receptacle — hide the volatility behind a socket you plug into). Three tests at once: ① domain-agnostic — no terminal/canvas/git/xterm leaks into the interface; ② hides a hard volatility — transport, reconnect, GPU-context loss, persistence — not a 3-line ===; ③ graduates — ships as @kolu/* and a different app plugs in. The proof of ③ is real, not aspirational. @kolu/surface is kolu's own client↔server transport first — and it has since picked up a second consumer in srid/drishti (a fleet process-monitor; browser ↔ Bun ↔ ssh-agent) that vendors it from this repo and declares its own per-host schema with none of kolu's domain. The second consumer isn't the point of the extraction — it's the proof it was real electricity: surface never noticed the domain changed.

“Decompose based on volatility.” Functional decomposition splits a system by what it does; volatility-based decomposition isolates each area likely to change behind a stable interface — the way a body keeps blood-pressure, pulse, and salinity volatility “encapsulated behind the service called the heart.” An electricity, here, is one such volatility pulled out into its own package. — Juval Löwy, Volatility-Based Decomposition (excerpt from Righting Software).

Next: extract @kolu/solid-xterm

The one genuine not-yet-built electricity — mis-filed at first as "terminal domain," but a SolidJS adapter for xterm.js is agnostic (the .agency/lowy.md §6.5 "what library is this?" test). It owns two declared volatility rows — async-init cleanup (the #598/#600 ~900 KB leaks) and canvas/WebGL lifecycle (the #591 zombie-context hunt, #575/#239/#595).

Inline today in one Terminal.tsx onMount (972 lines): new XTerm :472, 8× loadAddon :489-509, the WebGL single-owner dance :222-285, owner-correct async init :463-471, fit/resize :179/:750, touch-scroll :549-596.

Graduates the way surface did: any SolidJS app embedding xterm can plug in.
