Electricity proposals — the debated successor

A fresh, blind structural sweep, reconciled by a lowy⇄hickey debate into six actionable receptacle decisions. budding  captured 2026-06-01 · sibling of electricity-review.html

The electricity review ran a whole-app Hickey/Lowy audit and surfaced 16 candidates. Its "ship now" tier landed in #1089 — persistedPref, the @kolu/log + @kolu/html-escape leaf packages, and the dom/ + keybind relocations. This document is its actionable successor: a second, independent sweep run blind — the scouts and the lowy⇄hickey debate were never shown the prior audit's verdicts, precisely to avoid anchoring on them. The proposals below stand on that fresh consensus, not on the prior doc. We consult the audit only at the end (§ Continuity) to mark where the fresh pass confirms, refines, or updates the earlier plan. Where they differ, this doc updates the plan.

The five proposals

Each card carries the agreed disposition, the receptacle's shape, the braid it dissolves (with file:line evidence), and the one-line position of each lens lifted from its final reasoning. propose means extract a real new seam; fix-in-place means the right move is to unify an existing pattern or inline a false boundary — not to build anything.

propose createRefcountedSubscription persistence

packages/integrations/io/src/refcounted-dir-watcher.ts:63-151 · settled in 1 round

The braid today

The same refcounting frame — Map<K, listeners Set>, install on first subscribe, tear down on last unsubscribe, fault-isolated per-listener error handling, test-only reset hooks — is implemented twice and consumed six times:

  • refcounted-dir-watcher.ts:66-150 — Map<dir, SharedFilenameWatcher> with listeners Set; install/cleanup on first/last subscribe; per-listener try-catch at lines 86-93.
  • packages/shared/src/sqlite/wal-subscription.ts:99-149 — identical refcounting via let sharedWalWatcher: { cleanup, listeners: Set }; per-listener try-catch at lines 115-120; same snapshot-before-iteration (85 / 115).
  • Six module-scope instantiations: git head-watcher.ts:18, index-watcher.ts:18, reflog-watcher.ts:19, cwd-git-watcher.ts:20; db opencode/wal-watcher.ts:10, codex/wal-watcher.ts:10.

Proposed receptacle — the generic / specialization split

Extract a generic refcounting primitive (L0) in packages/shared/src/refcounted-subscription.ts, then reimplement the two watchers as thin specializations (L1) that supply only the domain-specific install callback:

// L0 — packages/shared/src/refcounted-subscription.ts
createRefcountedSubscription<K>(config: {
  install(key: K, onEvent: () => void, log?: Logger): (() => void) | null,
  onListenerError?(err: unknown, log?: Logger): void,
}) => {
  subscribe(key: K, onChange: () => void, log?: Logger): () => void,
  _count(key?: K): number,
  _reset(): void,
}

// L1 — specializations supply install():
//   dir-watcher  → (dir, onEvent, log) => { fs.watch + filename filter + debounce }
//   wal-subscription → the WAL + inode-replacement dance

What it dissolves

The refcounting lifecycle (when to install, when to tear down, error isolation) is completely orthogonal to the domain (fs.watch by name vs. WAL by inode). Extracting the generic unbraids that coupling and leaves domain logic — filename filtering, debounce, inode replacement — in its natural place.

lowy says

The repeating "refcounted subscription" shape across six call sites is the missing seam. Volatility = refcounting; stability = what resource you watch. The shared primitive belongs in @kolu/shared for package coherence.

hickey says

Don't extract the domain watcher as-is — extract the generic refcounting shape first. Identical Map/listeners/error-isolation logic in two places; six closure-private singletons follow the same pattern. Unbraid lifecycle from domain.

Risks / alternatives

This is the same server-side "wrote it thrice" smell the prior audit named — it sharpens that finding into a concrete L0/L1 split (see § Continuity). Risk: over-generalizing K or the install contract; keep it to exactly what the two specializations need. The test-only _count/_reset hooks must be preserved through the move.

fix-in-place isAbort text

packages/client/src/recorder/useRecorder.ts:142 · settled in 2 rounds

The braid today

An isAbort(err: unknown): boolean check is defined twice in the recorder module — useRecorder.ts:142 and webcam.ts:46 — and called at five user-action sites: useRecorder.ts openSetup:170, changeMic:181, startRecording:251 and webcam.ts toggleWebcam:81, changeWebcam:91.

The fix

Export isAbort from useRecorder.ts:142-144 and import it into webcam.ts:46, deleting the duplicate. All five call sites use the shared export. Leave isExpectedCleanupError() at rpc/streamCleanup.ts:7-9 alone — it hides a distinct volatility axis (framework-initiated RPC stream cleanup on unmount) used by Terminal.tsx and useTerminals.ts.

What it dissolves

Within-domain triplication of one helper — without merging two genuinely different error checks. Recorder-domain concerns (user cancellation, device switching) are encapsulated once; framework concerns (RPC teardown) stay separate.

lowy says

The recorder's axis (user-initiated media cancellation) is orthogonal to the RPC domain's axis (framework iterator cleanup). Dedup within the recorder; keep isExpectedCleanupError separate — they evolve independently.

hickey says

Genuine within-domain triplication: deduplicate per the simplicity principle. But the two checks serve different purposes and are consumed by separate hierarchies — neither lens supports merging them into one utility.

Risks / alternatives

Tempting over-reach: a single app-wide isAbortLike. Both lenses reject it — that would fuse two volatility axes ("user changed their mind" vs. "framework tore down the stream") behind one name.

fix-in-place useComposer → createSharedRoot reactive

packages/client/src/comments/composerState.ts:32 · settled in 1 round

The braid today

The candidate was a new stateSlot receptacle for guarded modal state — { get(), open(payload), close(), isActive() } — over four call sites in comments/ (useTextSelection.ts:123, CommentComposer.tsx:35, CommentIframeSurface.tsx, CodeTab.tsx). But the volatility is already encapsulated behind the useComposer() interface. The real problem is concept multiplication: the codebase has one canonical singleton pattern, createSharedRoot (packages/client/src/createSharedRoot.ts:23-33), used by useThemeManager, useIntentEditor, useTerminalStore, useModHeld, useDockOrder, and getNowTicker — yet useComposer hand-rolls a second way with module-scope createSignal. A stateSlot extraction would be a third.

The fix

Migrate useComposer() to createSharedRoot(): move the module-level createSignal init (composerState.ts:32) into a factory wrapped by createSharedRoot at the export (composerState.ts:34-51). Zero interface changes at the four call sites. No new boundary.

What it dissolves

The third singleton variant — by reifying the one pattern the project already declares. The guarded-modal volatility (documented at composerState.ts:1-12) stays behind the stable useComposer interface; only the implementation style changes.

lowy says

The boundary already exists and is stable under its axis; four tightly-coupled call sites are low-reuse but not disqualifying. Unify the implementation style, not the interface — zero new boundaries.

hickey says

Correctly diagnosed concept multiplication. createSharedRoot already solves this exact problem with no added complexity. Reject the extraction; reify the canonical pattern.

Risks / alternatives

Risk: createSharedRoot's root-disposal semantics must match the modal's current lifecycle (guards preventing stale pills and lost drafts). Verify the guarded transitions survive the migration before declaring done.

fix-in-place fanoutListeners reactive

packages/integrations/io/src/refcounted-dir-watcher.ts:73-130 · settled in 2 rounds

The braid today

The candidate was a monolithic debounceAndFanout receptacle. The real, narrow duplication is the 5–9 line error-isolated fanout loop (snapshot before iteration, try-catch per listener, contextual error logging) copied verbatim at three sites: refcounted-dir-watcher.ts:85-94, working-tree-watcher.ts:108-117, and repo-change.ts:60-69.

The fix

// co-exported micro-utility — packages/integrations/io/src/index.ts
fanoutListeners(
  listeners: Set<() => void> | Array<() => void>,
  logLabel: string,
  logFields: Record<string, unknown>,
  log?: Logger,
): void

Replace the three verbatim copies with calls. Each site keeps its own debounce timer, listener collection, upstream lifecycle (fs.watch / parcelSubscribe / composed upstreams), and pending coalescing (site 2's pending set at working-tree-watcher.ts:106-107).

What it dissolves

The drift risk of three hand-copied error-handling loops — without forcing the three sites' qualitatively different upstream strategies (sync fs.watch; async parcelSubscribe with pending coalescing + filePath matching; composed upstreams) behind one interface that couldn't survive them.

lowy says

A monolithic debounceAndFanout would NOT survive unchanged under three different upstream strategies — site 2 would have to abandon its pending optimization. Extract only the fanout loop as a micro-reuse helper, not a receptacle.

hickey says

Real duplication in the verbatim 5–9 line loop — a genuine bug risk if one copy drifts. Un-braid the error-handling concern without restructuring the sites. Micro-utility, same volatility axis (error isolation).

Risks / alternatives

This is the structural twin of proposal 2 at a finer grain: that one extracts the refcounting lifecycle; this one extracts only the fanout loop. Keep them distinct — collapsing both into one mega-helper is exactly the monolith both lenses rejected here.

fix-in-place compose → inline persistence

packages/integrations/git/src/repo-change.ts:46-90 · settled in 1 round

The braid today

A local compose function (repo-change.ts:46-90) bundles three concerns — refcount listeners + debounce + upstream teardown — and is called twice, in subscribeRepoChange (110-125) and subscribeFileChange (146-157). The same pattern already appears four times across the codebase at different abstraction levels: git compose(), working-tree installSharedWorkingTreeWatcher(), filesystem createDirFilenameWatcher(), and SQLite createWalSubscription().

The fix

Delete compose and inline it into the two call sites. Each inline block shows its three stages plainly: (1) listeners Set + debounce timer; (2) install all upstreams calling tick(); (3) a subscribe closure managing the refcount, firing upstream cleanup + onLast on the last unsubscribe. The composed upstreams and debounce window become visible at the call site.

What it dissolves

A false 2-site receptacle that fragments the real 4-instance duplication rather than solving it. Inlining keeps the code short (3–4 lines per site), makes the upstream array domain-visible, and preserves the option for a proper coordinated unification later — across all four instances at the architectural level (which proposal 2 begins).

lowy says

Not a cross-module receptacle — and the right action is to inline, not to keep a module-scope utility that hides no volatility and crosses no boundary. Conceding to Hickey: this is concept multiplication, not insufficient refactoring.

hickey says

compose is concept duplication; extracting it as a 2-site utility multiplies the concept across a package wall. Inline it; unify all four instances together later, not via a local utility.

Risks / alternatives

Reads as "un-DRY," but is deliberate: the architectural unification belongs in proposal 2's createRefcountedSubscription when all four sites are addressed together. Inlining here is the bridge — it stops the fragmentation without pre-committing to a shape.

Methodology

10 independent scouts→ blind dedup + drop-what-exists→ lowy ⇄ hickey debate→ per-finding consensus→ prior audit consulted (continuity only)

A fresh blind sweep: ten independent scouts hunted duplicated structure across the codebase without being shown the prior audit's verdicts. Their findings were de-duplicated and intersected against master to drop everything that already exists (the shipped table). Each survivor was then debated by the two lenses — Lowy's volatility-based decomposition (does this hide a real axis of change behind a stable interface?) and Hickey's structural simplicity (does this complect, or dissolve a braid?) — cross-examining every finding until they agreed per-finding. The agreed dispositions are the five proposals above.

The no-preseeding design is deliberate: had the scouts seen the audit, they'd have rediscovered its conclusions rather than tested them. The audit was opened only at the very end, to write § Continuity — so where the two passes agree, that agreement is genuine independent corroboration, and where they differ, the fresh consensus is the one that updates the plan. Grounded against Juval Lowy, Righting Software (the electricity receptacle metaphor) and Rich Hickey, Simple Made Easy.
