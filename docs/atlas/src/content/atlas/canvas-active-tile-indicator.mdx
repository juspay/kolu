The canvas tile now carries an **animated agent-status ring** on its border
(marching ants / sweeping comet, in the terminal's theme colour — see
[canvas-tile-state-borders](/canvas-tile-state-borders.html)). That ring, plus
the repo-identity border colour, means the **border is spoken for**. But the
*active / focused* tile is still marked the old way — a teal `--color-accent`
right-edge + a 1px accent ring — which now sits right on top of the status ring
and reads as a second, competing border.

<Callout kind="note" title="The ask">
The **active** indicator should be **completely different** from the agent-status
thing — its own channel, not another coloured border. (And the teal right-edge
has to go.)
</Callout>

Every tile below wears its **agent-status comet** (the spinning border light). The
**middle** tile is the focused one. The question is only how *focus* is marked.

## Today — and why it's out

<Pill variant="run">rejected</Pill> The focused tile gets a teal accent on its
right edge + a 1px accent ring. Sitting on the same border the status comet
travels, it reads as a **second border** fighting the first — exactly the clash
you flagged.

<AiGallery opt="today" lead="Today · teal right-edge + accent ring (competes with the status ring)" />

## The options

All three move focus **off the border** entirely, so the border belongs to repo
identity + agent status alone.

### A — Accent title bar

<Pill variant="good">Recommended</Pill> The focused tile's **title bar** lights
up in the accent (a tint + a thin accent underline). Focus lives in the *chrome*;
the border is never touched. Unmistakable, and it can't be confused with the
status ring because it's a different part of the tile. Bonus: the title bar is
where you read the tile's identity anyway, so the eye is already there.

<AiGallery opt="titlebar" lead="A · accent title bar — focus in the chrome (recommended)" />

### B — Lift / elevation

No colour at all: the focused tile **raises** (a stronger drop shadow) and the
others **recede** (dim). Pure depth — nothing can compete with the border because
focus isn't a border treatment at all. Calmest and most physical, but more subtle
across a busy canvas, and it leans on the inactive tiles dimming to read.

<AiGallery opt="lift" lead="B · lift + recede — depth only, no colour" />

### C — Outer accent glow

A soft accent **halo outside** the tile (plus a faint lift). Different geometry
from the on-border comet (it bleeds outward, not on the edge), so it doesn't
*overlap* the ring — but it still puts the accent colour near the border, so on a
dense canvas it can read as busy next to the comet. The middle ground.

<AiGallery opt="glow" lead="C · outer accent glow — accent, but off the edge" />

## Recommendation

<Callout kind="good" title="A — accent title bar, optionally with a hint of B">
**A** is the cleanest separation: the **border** = repo identity + agent status;
the **title bar** = focus. Two different surfaces, zero overlap, and the accent
colour never appears on the border. Pair it with a *gentle* version of **B** (the
focused tile lifts a touch, inactive tiles dim to ~0.92 as they already do) for
depth, and drop the teal right-edge and the accent ring from
<Cite file="packages/client/src/canvas/CanvasTile.tsx" /> entirely. **C** is the
fallback if you want the accent *somewhere* near the tile, but it's the most
likely to read busy beside the comet.
</Callout>
