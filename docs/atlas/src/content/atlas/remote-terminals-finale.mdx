**The last leg of <Issue n={951} /> ‚Äî kolu dials remote hosts.** R8 (<PrLink pr={1594} />) shipped the right **reader-join** (a terminal is `kolu.authored ‚ãà terminalWorkspace.awareness`, joined at the reader) ‚Äî *keep that*. The question this note settles is how kolu gets the **awareness** half for a *remote* host, and the answer turns out to already be in the repo: the awareness capability is a **decoupled library** ‚Äî `@kolu/pulam-library` ‚Äî and kolu already consumes it **in-process**. The only real defect is that the library is a *bag of pieces* with **no single assembly**, so the daemon and kolu each wire those pieces up themselves, divergently. This note corrects course: give the library **one assembly entry point** (a working name: createPulam), make the pulam **daemon** a trivial wrapper over it, have kolu consume that **same** assembly **in-process** for the local box (R9.0) and **mirror the remote pulam daemon** for a remote host (R9.3). The decoupling is the **library boundary**, not a process boundary. The parent [remote-terminals](remote-terminals.html) keeps the one-line rows; full model in [the terminal model](terminal-metadata-model.html).

<Callout kind="warn" title="The earlier correction was itself a misdiagnosis ‚Äî R9.0-as-a-process is CLOSED">
The prior plan (and PR #1614) said R8's mistake was that kolu *serves awareness in-process*, and proposed kolu run a **local pulam process** it mirrors over a socket. That was wrong on the diagnosis and the cure. **Grounded in the code:** `@kolu/pulam-library` already points its dependency arrow **out** (it names no kolu-app package; its lone host coupling is an injected logger) and already has six consumers ‚Äî it *is* the decoupling. kolu already consumes it in-process (`server/src/terminalEndpoint/local.ts` runs the library's `startAwareness` against kaval's taps). So "decouple awareness from kolu in code" was solved before R9.0. The local-pulam-**process** bought no decoupling and introduced a fold-clobber blocker that is intrinsic to mirroring an *ephemeral* local process across a socket. **PR #1614 is CLOSED (never merged); the process approach is rejected.** The pulam daemon is kept for the *remote* case, where a process boundary is real.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="note" title="The real gap ‚Äî two assemblers, assembled differently">
The library exposed the *parts* ‚Äî the sensor engine (`startAwareness`) and the sink **interface** (`AwarenessSink`), the kaval-tap bridge (`bridgeKavalTaps`), the schema/seeds, the served-surface factory (`serveTerminalWorkspace`), the surface contract, the fs/git endpoint ‚Äî but **not** the *whole*: "given a dialed kaval, produce a live, served terminalWorkspace surface." Before R9¬∑lib that **assembly** lived in the pulam **daemon** (`runPulamDaemon`), with two generic pieces stranded there ‚Äî the per-terminal sink and the byte-tap activity tracker. **R9¬∑lib (below, shipped) closed the lib‚Üîdaemon half**: the assembly is now one library entry point (`createPulam`), the daemon a thin wrapper, the two pieces lifted into the library (`awarenessSink.ts` / `activity.ts`). What's still open is kolu's *own* divergent subset of that same assembly inside `local.ts` ‚Äî **two assemblers, assembled differently**, the root of the local-vs-remote divergence; **R9.0** routes kolu onto `createPulam` to close it.
</Callout>

## The sub-phases

<a id="r9lib"></a>

### R9¬∑lib ‚Äî consolidate the pulam-library (one assembly, lib‚Üîdaemon 1:1) ‚Äî *shipped* <PrLink pr={1617} />

<Phase id="R9¬∑lib" name="one library assembly entry point ‚Äî createPulam; the daemon wraps it" status="shipped" needs={["R6", "R4"]} links={[{ label: "#1617", href: "https://github.com/juspay/kolu/pull/1617" }]} />

**The gap R9¬∑lib closed, grounded.** The library (`@kolu/pulam-library`) is genuinely decoupled ‚Äî its dependency arrow points **out** (`package.json`: `@kolu/surface`, kaval, the agent/git integrations; no kolu-app dependency) and it has **six** consumers (kolu-server, pulam, pulam-web, pulam-tui, common, solid-statepip). But before this phase it exported *parts*, not a *whole*:

- the sensor engine `startAwareness` and the **interface** `AwarenessSink` (`sensors.ts:849`, `sensors.ts:163`),
- the kaval-tap bridge `bridgeKavalTaps` (`kavalChannels.ts:58`),
- the surface-skeleton factory `serveTerminalWorkspace` + `quietActivity` (`serveTerminalWorkspace.ts:73`, `serveTerminalWorkspace.ts:53`),
- the surface contract `terminalWorkspaceSurface` and the fs/git endpoint `createTerminalWorkspaceEndpoint`.

What it did **not** export was the assembly that turns *a dialed kaval* into *a live, served terminalWorkspace surface*. That assembly lived in the **pulam daemon** (`runPulamDaemon` in `pulam/src/daemon.ts`): dial kaval ‚Üí a per-terminal `watchTerminal` wired `seedAwarenessValue` ‚Üí its own `makeAwarenessSink` ‚Üí `bridgeKavalTaps` ‚Üí `startAwareness` ‚Üí a raw-output activity tap ‚Üí and a `reconcile` kept the set in step, all fed into `serveTerminalWorkspace`. Two generic pieces were stranded **in the daemon package**: the per-terminal sink `makeAwarenessSink` (`pulam/src/hooks.ts`) and the byte-tap `createActivityTracker` (`pulam/src/activity.ts`). **R9¬∑lib moved all three into the library** ‚Äî `createPulam` (`pulam-library/src/createPulam.ts`) now owns the assembly, with the sink and tap-tracker lifted across to `awarenessSink.ts` + `activity.ts`; `pulam/src/hooks.ts` is gone and the daemon dropped 390‚Üí231 lines.

kolu re-implements a **divergent** subset of that same assembly:

| piece | the library / daemon | kolu-server | the duplication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| the sink | `pulam/src/hooks.ts:39` (publishes the whole value into the served collection) | `local.ts:278` (writes the registry projection ¬∑ `trackRecentRepo`/`trackRecentAgent` ¬∑ screen-scrape off the local handle) | two impls of one `AwarenessSink` interface |
| the kaval-tap bridge | `bridgeKavalTaps` (`kavalChannels.ts:58`) | the four channels built **inline** (`local.ts:688-744`) | kolu reaches kaval over the same socket-backed `ptyHostClient` facade (`local.ts:4`), so its inline copy collapses onto `bridgeKavalTaps` ‚Äî re-adding its own `signals.cwd` consumer for the persisted-cwd write (`local.ts:716`), exactly as the daemon does (`daemon.ts:237`); the library's docstring (`kavalChannels.ts:6`) still calls kolu's feed "in-process," now stale |
| live activity | `createActivityTracker` (`pulam/src/activity.ts:43`), served live | `quietActivity` (`surface.ts:458`); the green dot comes from client-side `useTerminalActivity` | one byte-tap in the daemon, a second on the client; kolu's server-side activity is quiet |

**The fix ‚Äî one assembly, two trivial homes.** Lift the assembly into the library behind one entry point ‚Äî a working name, createPulam(kavalClient) ‚Äî that owns the per-terminal sensor lifecycle (seed ‚Üí sink ‚Üí tap-bridge ‚Üí sensors ‚Üí activity tap ‚Üí reconcile) and returns the `serveTerminalWorkspace` deps, with the **sink's write target injected by the home** (the same shape `serveTerminalWorkspace` already uses to inject the awareness backing). Move `makeAwarenessSink` and `createActivityTracker` **into the library**. Then:

- the **pulam daemon** collapses to a trivial shell ‚Äî it dials a kaval, builds createPulam(kaval), and serves the result over a unix socket (or stdio, for ssh);
- **kolu** calls createPulam against its own kaval client, in-process, injecting "write into the registry record" as the sink target ‚Äî so its persisted/live fence and the reader-join are unchanged (R9.0).

One assembler, in the library; the daemon and kolu are both thin. That is the 1:1 ‚Äî **library ‚Üî daemon** ‚Äî and it is what makes R9.0 (in-process) and R9.3 (mirror the daemon) two faces of the *same* surface.

<Callout kind="note" title="Open design calls this phase pins (don't invent them mid-flight)">
- **Where the in-process sink writes.** kolu's awareness must keep landing on its **registry** entry (so `composeTerminalMetadata`, the persisted/live fence, and session-restore hold). createPulam must take the sink's write target as an injected dep ‚Äî registry-mutation for kolu, an owned store for the daemon ‚Äî exactly the injection `serveTerminalWorkspace` already does for the `awareness` backing. No fold: in-process the sink mutates the restored record directly, with no socket reseed.
- **The per-terminal lifecycle.** kolu's lifecycle is entangled with spawn/sleep/wake/adopt (`local.ts`, ~1100 lines); the daemon's is a plain poll-list reconcile. The *minimum* 1:1 is one sink + one tap-bridge + one activity tracker in the library, both homes assembling from those. Whether to also lift the whole watch/reconcile loop into createPulam (vs leave kolu's lifecycle as the caller of the shared pieces) is the scope call this phase makes.
- **Activity.** Decide whether kolu's server-side activity source goes live off the shared byte-tap (retiring the client-side `useTerminalActivity`) or stays client-side. This phase only *moves the tracker into the library*; flipping kolu's source live is a follow-on, not a freebie.
- **Rename.** terminal-workspace ‚Üí pulam-library ‚Äî the library *is* the pulam capability; the daemon is one deployment of it. The rename rides this phase (mechanical, ~6 consumers).
</Callout>

- **Needs:** R6 (the library exists) and R4 (the pulam daemon) ‚Äî **both shipped**.
- **Done:** there is **one** assembly entry point in the library (`createPulam`); the pulam daemon is a thin serve-wrapper over it (no awareness logic of its own, 390‚Üí231 lines); `makeAwarenessSink` and `createActivityTracker` no longer live in the pulam package (moved to `awarenessSink.ts` / `activity.ts` in the library); a **structural** guard (`pulam/src/daemon.assembler.test.ts`) parses `daemon.ts`'s imports and forbids the awareness-assembly primitives, making a second assembler in the daemon **unspellable**. (Pinning the daemon's assembly and kolu's in-process one as the *same* code path by a **differential** test is **R9.0**, when kolu cuts over onto `createPulam` ‚Äî see below.)

### R9.0 ‚Äî kolu consumes the pulam-library in-process ¬∑ *the decouple completes*

<Phase id="R9.0" name="kolu consumes the pulam-library in-process" status="todo" needs={["R9¬∑lib"]} />

**The point.** Once R9¬∑lib gives the library one assembly entry point, kolu's local awareness is just createPulam against its own kaval client, called **in-process** ‚Äî no daemon, no socket, no mirror, **no fold**. kolu deletes its hand-rolled sink (`local.ts:278`) and its inline tap-bridge (`local.ts:688-744`) and consumes the library's assembly instead, injecting "write into the registry record" as the sink target. The reader-join stays exactly as R8 shipped it: the browser still subscribes to `terminalWorkspace.awareness ‚ãà kolu.authored` and joins via `composeTerminalMetadata`. The arrow points **into the library**, in-process ‚Äî which is not re-coupling, because the library knows nothing of kolu (its dependency arrow points the other way).

This is the in-process face of the **same** surface R9.3 mirrors remotely. It does not start a second process, and it does not change what the browser reads ‚Äî only how kolu's awareness collection is *backed* (now one library assembly rather than a bespoke in-line wiring).

- **Migration:** a **hard cutover** ‚Äî one change deletes kolu's bespoke sink + inline bridge and routes through the library's assembly, no flag and no fallback (the no-knobs rule; the worst case on the local awareness path is a briefly-stale badge, never lost work).
- **Needs:** R9¬∑lib.
- **Done:** kolu-server runs **no** bespoke awareness assembly ‚Äî its local awareness is the library's createPulam assembly called in-process; the bespoke sink and inline tap-bridge in `local.ts` are gone; the reader-join and disk persistence are unchanged; a test pins kolu's in-process awareness equal to what the pulam daemon would serve for the same terminals.

### R9.3 ‚Äî remote awareness = mirror the remote pulam daemon

<Phase id="R9.3" name="remote awareness ‚Äî mirror the remote pulam daemon's surface" status="todo" needs={["R9.0", "R9.2", "R7"]} />

A remote host runs the **pulam daemon** ‚Äî which, after R9¬∑lib, is the same library assembly (createPulam) served over a socket. So kolu's remote awareness backing is just **a mirror of that daemon's served surface**, dialed over a long-lived `getHostSession` and mirrored via R7's total dual. It is the wire-side face of the **same** surface kolu consumes in-process locally (R9.0): the contract (terminalWorkspaceSurface), the reader-join, the authored half, and disk persistence are identical ‚Äî **only the source differs** (an in-process call vs a mirror over ssh). The remote tile gains the git/agent/PR badge + green dot; `activity` rides the mirror.

This is also where the genuinely **remote-only** reconciliation lives: a restored remote tile's persisted awareness (git, lastActivityAt) must survive a fresh daemon re-deriving it across the wire. That fold belongs here, on the remote backing, **not** in the local in-process path (where there is no socket and no reseed).

- **Needs:** R9.0 (the in-process consumer proves the surface), R9.2 (the remote PTY), R7 (the total dual).
- **Done:** a remote tile shows live awareness mirrored from the remote pulam daemon, joined by the **same** reader-join as local; a differential test pins it equal to the remote pulam's own `awareness`.

### R9.4 ‚Äî persisted location, reconnect, adoption

<Phase id="R9.4" name="persisted location + reconnect + adoption" status="todo" needs={["R9.2"]} />
